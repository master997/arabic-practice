# Egyptian Arabic Practice

Everything you need before (and after) your first lesson.

## How to use

Open [index.html](index.html) in any browser — double-click it, or on your phone AirDrop the folder and open the file. No internet needed. Progress saves automatically per device. One calm warm-dark look, four buttons across the top: **Today · Reviews · Hard · What to do**.

- **Today** is where you start every day. It tells you exactly what to do in three steps — clear your reviews, learn a few new cards from an auto-rotating focus deck, then say them out loud. No deciding, just work down the list. It keeps a streak counter and a grid of your daily completions, so showing up is visible and satisfying.
- **What to do** is the plain-language guide (the daily routine) **and** where you paste your weekly words — see below. New here? Open it first.
- **Reviews** is your daily driver: every card that's come back due across all decks, drained as you clear it. Open the app, clear Reviews, done.
- **Hard** collects any card you tap the ★ on — your tricky words in one place.
- Tap a card (or press Space) to flip · **1** = again · **2** = got it · arrow keys to move.
- **Spaced repetition is built in (FSRS).** Mark **got it** and the card comes back on a schedule the app computes from how well it's sticking — a freshly-learned card returns *within the same session* (~10 min), then spaces out each time you recall it. Mark **again** and it drops back to a short step. Same modern algorithm Anki uses; ~20–30% fewer reviews than a fixed ladder for the same memory.
- Every card shows **Egyptian pronunciation** when flipped (say it first, then check): `3` = ع, `'` = hamza or Egyptian ق, `g` = ج. Careful: Quranic habits give wrong Egyptian — كتير is *keteer* not *katheer*, قهوة is *'ahwa* not *qahwa*.
- The back of a card also shows its **root** and other words from the same family (e.g. ر و ح → رايح, اروح), turning lone words into clusters.
- **AR → EN** (top right) flips the direction — do both; EN → AR (production) is the one that makes you able to *speak*.
- Need a specific topic? The 16 subject decks live under **Browse a topic** at the bottom of **What to do**. Your original binder pages are in [sheets/](sheets/).

## Adding your weekly words (you never fill in fields)

The whole point: **you paste, Claude does the pronunciation, root, example and tags.** Two ways in:

**1. The good way — let Claude enrich (recommended).** Paste this week's words into [inbox.md](inbox.md), one per line, however rough:

```
عايز        I want
بكام؟       how much?
لسه بدري    it's still early
```

Then ask Claude: **"process my inbox."** Claude reads `inbox.md`, writes each as a clean card — Egyptian pronunciation (`3`=ع, `'`=hamza/ق, `g`=ج), the three-letter root, a natural example sentence, topic tags — skips anything you already have, and clears the file. Open the app and the words are there, fully formed, in **My words** and **Reviews**.

**2. The offline way — quick save.** On your phone with no Claude around, open **What to do**, and in the box there paste `arabic | english`, one per line. The words save instantly so you can study them right away; next time you run the inbox, Claude backfills the pronunciation, root and example for them automatically.

**Backup:** the *Copy my words (backup)* button in **What to do** exports everything you've added, so your words are portable and never trapped on one device.

Your words are keyed by a stable id, not by the Arabic text — so cleaning up or editing a card never loses its review history.

## The daily loop (this is the main thing now)

Open the app → **Today** → do the three steps → done. The focus deck rotates on its own so over a couple of weeks you cycle through everything, then it loops. Your streak grows every day all three steps go green. That's the whole system — the plan below is only for the run-up to a first lesson.

## 7-day routine before the lesson (~20 min/day)

| Day | Focus |
|-----|-------|
| 1 | Classroom survival deck — these phrases let the lesson itself run in Arabic |
| 2 | Greetings & intros, then Small talk |
| 3 | Top 100 words, first half (AR → EN) |
| 4 | Top 100 words, second half, then redo yesterday's EN → AR |
| 5 | Sentence patterns + Top 25 verbs — say each example **aloud**, then invent one of your own per pattern |
| 6 | Conversation islands — rehearse aloud until each flows without thinking |
| 7 | Hard deck + Question drills, then one full pass of Classroom survival EN → AR |

Each day, start by clearing **Reviews** (your scheduled reviews come back automatically now — no need to manually redo old decks), then learn new cards from that day's deck.

Every day: say cards **out loud**, not in your head. Ten minutes aloud beats an hour of silent reading.

## Grammar quick-wins (90% of what you need on day one)

- **Negation:** `مش` before adjectives/participles (مش فاهم = I don't understand); `ما...ش` wraps verbs (معنديش = I don't have, متقلقش = don't worry)
- **Present:** `بـ` prefix — بشتغل (I work), بتتكلم (you speak)
- **Future:** `هـ` prefix — هروح (I'll go), هتخلص (you'll finish)
- **No "to be" in the present:** انا تعبان = I (am) tired — just leave it out

## Listening / shadowing (do a little daily)

- **Easy Arabic** (YouTube) — Egyptian street interviews with subtitles; pause and repeat aloud (shadowing)
- **Pimsleur Egyptian Arabic** — audio course, great for commutes
- **EgyptianArabic.com** — dialect-specific reference
- Any Egyptian series on Netflix — even with English subs, your ear tunes to the rhythm

## First-lesson game plan

1. **Open in Arabic** with your conversation islands (name, from England, learning Arabic).
2. Ask the tutor to give classroom instructions **in Arabic from day one** — you have the survival phrases for it.
3. Ask them to correct **naturalness and word choice** (Egyptian vs MSA habits) rather than every small grammar slip.
4. End by agreeing exactly what to prepare for lesson 2.

Tutor time is your scarcest resource — walking in with this agenda roughly doubles its value.

## Corrections to the printed sheets

- صباح النور is the *reply* to صباح الخير, not a greeting you open with (same for مساء النور).
- عندك كام سنة = "how old are you" (lit. "you have how many years"); انا عندي تلاتين سنة = "I'm 30".
- "I'm making coffee" = بعمل قهوة; the handwritten باكل on that page means "I'm eating".
